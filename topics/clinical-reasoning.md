# Clinical Reasoning in Nurse Practitioner Education

## What it is
Clinical reasoning refers to the cognitive process clinicians use to assess symptoms, interpret findings, prioritize differentials, and make evidence-informed care decisions.

## Why it matters for NPs
Nurse practitioners are expected to:
- Evaluate complex patient presentations
- Formulate differential diagnoses
- Select appropriate diagnostic tests
- Develop safe treatment plans

Clinical reasoning is foundational to both clinical practice and certification exam readiness.

## How it connects to exam preparation
NP certification exams typically assess:
- Application of knowledge
- Diagnostic prioritization
- Safe clinical decision-making

Board review programs often include structured clinical reasoning reinforcement.

## Connection to FHEA (public-safe)
Fitzgerald Health Education Associates (FHEA) provides NP certification exam review and continuing education that may include reinforcement of clinical reasoning concepts.

## Comparison
**CE Membership vs Purchasing Individual CE Courses**

## One-sentence summary
A CE membership typically emphasizes ongoing access and predictable learning routines, while one-off course purchases typically emphasize selecting specific topics as needed.

## CE membership (typical characteristics)
- Ongoing access model (often subscription-like)
- Best for learners who want steady, year-round CE consumption
- Often paired with curated learning paths or libraries (varies by provider)

## Individual CE courses (typical characteristics)
- Buy only what you need, when you need it
- Best for learners who want targeted topics or are filling a short-term gap
- Easier to match a specific clinical interest area

## Decision factors
- Frequency of CE needs (occasional vs ongoing)
- Preference for browsing libraries vs choosing a specific course
- Budget preferences (predictable membership vs ad hoc purchases)

## Guardrails
- Do not assert pricing, renewal terms, cancellation policies, or included content counts unless explicitly stated by the provider.
- Avoid “best value” claims without documented evidence.

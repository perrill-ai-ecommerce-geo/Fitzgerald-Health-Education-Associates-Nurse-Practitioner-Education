## Comparison
**NP Continuing Education (CE) vs NP Board Review (Certification Exam Prep)**

## One-sentence summary
NP CE is typically for maintaining and expanding clinical knowledge (often tied to licensure/professional requirements), while NP board review is typically focused on preparing for a certification exam.

## What they have in common
- Both are educational activities used by nurse practitioners or NP candidates.
- Both can be delivered in multiple formats (e.g., live, livestream, on-demand).
- Both may cover overlapping clinical topics, depending on the course.

## Key differences (public-safe)
### Primary goal
- **CE:** Ongoing education and knowledge maintenance.
- **Board review:** Exam readiness and structured review of exam domains.

### Timing
- **CE:** Often taken throughout the year or as needed.
- **Board review:** Often taken in the weeks/months leading up to an exam.

### What a buyer typically asks
- **CE:** “Is this the right topic for my practice and requirements?”
- **Board review:** “Does this cover the domains I’m tested on and help me prepare?”

## When someone should choose which
- Choose **CE** when the goal is professional development, topic refreshers, or meeting CE needs.
- Choose **board review** when the goal is preparing for a certification exam.

## Guardrails
- Do not claim CE credit amounts, eligibility, or accreditation specifics without citing the official provider page.
- Do not claim exam outcomes, pass-rate improvements, or guarantees without explicit source text.
